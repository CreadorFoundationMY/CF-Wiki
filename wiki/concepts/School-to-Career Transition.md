# School-to-Career Transition

The critical period during which secondary-school students must translate academic experiences into career choices, post-secondary pathway decisions, and early professional identity.

## Malaysian Context

In Malaysia, the key inflection point is **SPM** (Sijil Pelajaran Malaysia), the national secondary-school leaving examination taken at Form 5 (~age 17). Post-SPM pathways include:
- Pre-university programmes (STPM, A-Levels, Foundation, Matriculation)
- TVET (Technical and Vocational Education and Training)
- Direct employment

## The Problem Schola Addresses

From [[Creador Foundation]]'s study of 1,000+ students:
- **80%** lack essential knowledge about their chosen career.
- **70%** do not actively research career options.
- The majority only begin seriously planning post-SPM pathways **after** SPM results are released — by which time options may be constrained.

This late engagement is the core market failure [[Schola]] targets: building career awareness and aspiration **during** secondary school rather than reactively after exam results.

## Intervention Design Principles

Schola's design reflects several evidence-based principles:
1. **Exposure breadth**: Subject-to-Job Match surfaces unfamiliar careers alongside familiar ones.
2. **Concrete information**: Job Guides provide salary data, pathways, and expert testimony rather than generic descriptions.
3. **Gamification**: Gaji Goals Game makes abstract salary trajectories visceral and comparable.
4. **Personality anchoring**: [[RIASEC Holland Codes]] quiz gives students a self-concept to attach career exploration to.
