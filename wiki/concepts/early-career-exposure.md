# Early Career Exposure

"Early career exposure" is Schola's stated north star: help students encounter and understand careers before they make education-pathway decisions.

## Problem signals
Schola's research with roughly 1,000 surveyed students and 50 in-depth interviews found that many students:
- Have limited exposure to the range of careers available.
- Delay career research until deadlines are close.
- Rely on familiar professions, family, school, friends, and visible social circles.
- Struggle to connect education pathways to jobs and to contextualize salary information.

The deck reports headline survey findings:
- 8 in 10 lacked essential knowledge about their chosen careers.
- 7 in 10 did not actively research career options.
- 6 in 10 made choices based on short-term or familiarity factors.

## Gap identified
Students used schools, family, and online sources for post-SPM education information, then later used online platforms and social media for job vacancies. Schola identified a gap between education-pathway decisions and later job search: structured career exploration before students choose pathways.
