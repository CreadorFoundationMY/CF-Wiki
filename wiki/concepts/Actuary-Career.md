# Actuary Career (Malaysia)

Career concept page based on [[entities/Schola]]'s job guide for the Actuary role.

**BM name:** Aktuari  
**Industry:** Banking & Finance  
**Degree required:** Yes

## What Actuaries Do

Actuaries use maths and statistics to predict financial risks and advise companies on how to prepare for future uncertainties. Day-to-day work involves:
- Heavy use of Microsoft Excel; some coding
- Applying mathematical, statistical, and financial theory to analyse data
- Predicting risks and advising on financial preparation
- Helping organisations make informed financial decisions

## Salary Progression (Malaysia, 2024 estimates)

| Level | Salary (RM/month) | Experience |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Early career | 2,800–7,000 | 0 yrs |
| Supervisory | 3,500–9,000 | ~2 yrs |
| Managerial | 8,000–19,000 | ~6 yrs |
| Leadership (Appointed Actuary) | 30,000–75,000+ | ~15 yrs |

Each actuarial exam passed can add up to RM7,000 to salary (per [[entities/Anisha-Puri]]).

## Education & Certification

**Two pathways:**
1. **Degree pathway** (4–6 years): SPM → Pre-U → Actuarial Science degree → enter as Actuarial Associate
2. **Professional certification pathway** (7–13 years): SPM → Pre-U → Any degree → Actuarial Exams (IFoA or SOA, 3–7 years) → enter as Certified Actuary

An Actuarial Science degree is preferred but not mandatory — any degree works if professional exams are passed. Actuarial Science graduates may receive exemptions.

## Demand

- High demand in insurance companies and consulting firms in Malaysia
- Global opportunities open up post-certification
- Skills transferable to fintech and business analytics
