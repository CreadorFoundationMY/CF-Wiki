# Career Guidance in Malaysia

## Problem statement
"You can't be what you don't know." — Schola / [[creador-foundation]]

Access to career information in Malaysia is unequal. Students with professional networks or well-resourced schools have richer exposure to career options; those without are limited to a narrow set of visible role models.

## Research findings (Creador Foundation study, n > 1,000 students)

| Finding | Statistic |
|---------|-----------|
| Students with career ambitions who lack essential knowledge about their chosen field | **80%** |
| Students who do not actively research career options | **70%** |
| Timing of serious post-SPM pathway consideration | Most students begin only **after SPM** results |

## Implications
- Students enter post-secondary decisions under-informed, increasing risk of mismatch.
- The window to intervene meaningfully is **during secondary school**, before SPM.
- Passive ambition (having a dream career) is common; active exploration is rare.

## Schola's response
[[schola]] addresses this by providing free, self-directed, digital career discovery tools specifically designed for the Malaysian SPM cohort — covering career personalities, subject-to-job mapping, salary data, education pathways, and gamified financial literacy around salaries.
