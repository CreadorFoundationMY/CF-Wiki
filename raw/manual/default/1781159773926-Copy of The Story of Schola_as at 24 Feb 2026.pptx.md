### Speaker notes

After 50 in-depth interviews with about 50 young people, consisting of Form 4 & 5, Diploma, TVET student, Degree students, young gig workers, coupled with a survey with about 1,000 students.
We mapped out the journey of a typical student, from Form 4 to uni graduation, ready to apply for their first job. We realised that we were quite far off from our initial hypothesis.
We discovered that Form 4-5 students primarily relied on schools, family, and the internet for information on post-SPM education pathways. And the next thing they know, they were relying on the internet to search for jobs after graduation.
There was a significant gap in career exploration tools between the decision-making stage for education pathways and the job search stage after graduation.
Many students didn't prioritize career research, believing there was no immediate need and postponing these decisions until later.
Many students lacked knowledge about diverse careers and had limited exposure to them before choosing their educational paths.
Most students relied on familiarity, such as their parents' occupations, extended relatives, or the experiences of their seniors, to inform their career choices. They only knew typical jobs like lawyer, doctor, teacher, police.
This reliance on familiarity hindered their ability to explore unconventional or unfamiliar career paths. Students can’t aspire to be what they don’t know.
Given the existing resources for educational pathways and job vacancies, we've identified a critical gap in career exploration.

## Slide 15

HOW SCHOLA WORKS
…now we think of it as an ecosystem with 5 engines
DISTRIBUTION CHANNELS
01 — SOCIAL MEDIA
Makes careers feel interesting, then earns the click.
We use social to pull students in before they would ever search for careers. It’s where we build curiosity, and make unfamiliar jobs feel less intimidating.
17M views ·55k followers ·92k engagements
02 — PROGRAMMES
Builds the trust that makes scale real
This engine helps Schola show up in real school moments, and reach students who would never find us through search or social alone.
4000teachers ·14key stakeholders ·2200counsellor subscribers
▶
WHERECORE LEARNING HAPPENS
schola.org.my—The website.
03 — PRODUCT
The tools that make exploration stick.
Our tools help students explore beyond what they already know, and give structure to decisions that usually feel vague.
Career Quiz · Subject Lens · Directory · Salary View
04 — CONTENT
The knowledge backbone thatmakes the website credible
Every guide is built on real interviews with real professionals.
150+ guides ·250+ expert interviews ·
4.5 guides per student
05 — DATA & ANALYTICS
The learning loop that tells us what to fix, build, and push next.
We use it to see where students drop off, what they ignore, what they return to, and which entry points actually lead to deeper exploration. It drives decisions across all four engines.
THE DECISION WE MADE EARLY
Why we keep learning on the website
Social and programmes are designed to bring students to the website because that’s the only place we can give a consistent experience, personalise the journey, and learn from what students actually do.
A 60-second video or a one-off school session can spark interest, but it cannot replace structured exploration, or show us what students clicked, skipped, and came back to.
